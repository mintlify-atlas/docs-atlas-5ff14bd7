---
title: "Payments"
description: "Record customer receipts, supplier payments, and internal transfers in Frappe Books, and reconcile them against outstanding invoices."
---

A Payment in Frappe Books records the movement of money between your company and a party (customer or supplier). Payments update the outstanding amounts on linked invoices and post ledger entries that reflect the cash or bank movement.

## Payment types

Frappe Books supports two payment directions:

| Payment Type | When to use | Party role |
|---|---|---|
| **Receive** | Money coming in from a customer | Customer or Both |
| **Pay** | Money going out to a supplier | Supplier or Both |

<Info>
Frappe Books does not have a separate "Internal Transfer" type. To transfer between two bank or cash accounts, create a Journal Entry with a Contra Entry type, debiting the destination account and crediting the source account.
</Info>

## How payments affect the ledger

The `getPosting` method in the Payment model defines the double-entry posting:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Receive (from customer)">
    When you receive a payment from a customer:

    | Account | Direction | Example |
    |---|---|---|
    | To Account (`paymentAccount`) | **Debit** | Cash / Bank |
    | From Account (`account`) | **Credit** | Debtors (Receivable) |

    Cash or Bank increases (debit), and the customer's receivable balance decreases (credit).
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Pay (to supplier)">
    When you pay a supplier:

    | Account | Direction | Example |
    |---|---|---|
    | To Account (`paymentAccount`) | **Debit** | Creditors (Payable) |
    | From Account (`account`) | **Credit** | Cash / Bank |

    The supplier's payable balance decreases (debit), and your Cash or Bank decreases (credit).
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Create a payment

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Payment">
    Go to **Accounting** in the sidebar and select **Payment**, then click **New**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the payment type">
    Choose **Receive** (customer payment) or **Pay** (supplier payment) from the **Payment Type** field. This controls which accounts are available in the account fields and which party roles are shown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the party">
    In the **Party** field, search for and select the customer or supplier. Frappe Books filters the party list based on the Payment Type:
    - **Receive**: shows parties with role `Customer` or `Both`
    - **Pay**: shows parties with role `Supplier` or `Both`

    Selecting a party automatically determines the payment direction and pre-fills the **From Account** with the party's default receivable or payable account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the posting date">
    The **Posting Date** defaults to today. Change it to match the actual date the payment was received or made.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the payment method">
    Select the **Payment Method** (defaults to `Cash`). The available methods are configured under **Setup > Payment Method**. Each method has a type:

    | Method Type | Behaviour |
    |---|---|
    | **Cash** | To Account filters to Cash-type accounts |
    | **Bank** | To Account filters to Bank-type accounts. Requires Clearance Date and Ref. / Cheque No. |
    | **Transfer** | Electronic transfer; To Account filters to Bank or Cash accounts |

  </Step>
  <Step title="Set account fields">
    | Field | Label in UI | Description |
    |---|---|---|
    | `account` | **From Account** | The party-side account. For Receive: Receivable (Debtors). For Pay: Cash or Bank. |
    | `paymentAccount` | **To Account** | The cash/bank-side account. For Receive: Cash or Bank. For Pay: Payable (Creditors). |

    Both accounts are auto-populated based on the Payment Type and Payment Method, but you can override them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the amount">
    Set the **Amount** — the total payment received or made. If you link invoices in the next step, the amount is automatically set to the sum of the linked invoice amounts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Link to invoices (optional)">
    In the **Payment Reference** section (`for` table), add one row per invoice you want to apply this payment to:

    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Type** | `Sales` (for Sales Invoices) or `Purchase` (for Purchase Invoices) |
    | **Name** | The invoice number (dynamically linked based on Type) |
    | **Amount** | The portion of this payment to apply to this invoice |

    When you add a row, Frappe Books validates that:
    - The invoice belongs to the selected party.
    - The payment amount does not exceed the invoice's outstanding amount.
    - If partial payment is not enabled in settings, the full outstanding amount must be paid.

    <Tip>
    Adding an invoice reference automatically sets the Party and Payment Type if they are not already set, saving you a step.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add reference details (for bank payments)">
    If the Payment Method type is **Bank**, you must fill in:

    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Ref. / Cheque No.** (`referenceId`) | The cheque number, wire transfer reference, or transaction ID |
    | **Reference Date** | The date on the cheque or bank transaction |
    | **Clearance Date** | The date the payment cleared in your bank account |

  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply a write-off (optional)">
    If the payment amount is slightly less than the invoice outstanding (e.g. due to bank charges or currency rounding), set the **Write Off** amount. Frappe Books posts the write-off difference to the Write Off Account configured in **Accounting Settings**.

    The **Amount Paid** field (read-only) shows `Amount − Write Off`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the payment">
    Click **Submit**. Frappe Books:
    - Posts the debit and credit ledger entries.
    - Reduces the outstanding amount on each linked invoice by the allocated amount.
    - Updates the party's total outstanding balance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Payment field reference

| Field | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `name` | Data | Payment number (auto-generated from series `PAY-`) |
| `numberSeries` | Link | Number series for auto-numbering |
| `party` | Link | Customer or supplier |
| `date` | Datetime | Posting date |
| `paymentType` | Select | `Receive` or `Pay` |
| `account` | Link | From Account (receivable for Receive; cash/bank for Pay) |
| `paymentAccount` | Link | To Account (cash/bank for Receive; payable for Pay) |
| `paymentMethod` | Link | Cash, Bank, or Transfer |
| `clearanceDate` | Date | Bank clearance date (required for Bank method) |
| `referenceId` | Data | Cheque or transaction reference number |
| `referenceDate` | Date | Date on the cheque or transaction |
| `amount` | Currency | Total payment amount |
| `writeoff` | Currency | Amount written off (small balance clearance) |
| `amountPaid` | Currency | `Amount − Write Off` (computed) |
| `taxes` | Table | Tax summary (computed from linked invoices) |
| `for` | Table | Invoice references (type, name, amount) |
| `attachment` | Attachment | Supporting document (bank receipt, etc.) |

## Payment methods

Payment methods are configured under **Setup > Payment Method**. Each method has:

- **Name**: The display name (e.g. Cash, HDFC Bank, Stripe)
- **Type**: `Cash`, `Bank`, or `Transfer`
- **Account**: The default bank or cash account to use as the To Account when this method is selected

You can create as many payment methods as you have bank accounts or payment channels.

## Reconciliation and outstanding invoices

When you submit a payment linked to one or more invoices, Frappe Books automatically reduces the **Outstanding Amount** on each linked invoice. You can see the payment history on any invoice in its **Outstanding** section.

To view all outstanding invoices for a party:

1. Open the party record (Customer or Supplier).
2. The party's **Outstanding Amount** field shows the net balance across all unpaid invoices.
3. Use the **Accounts Receivable** or **Accounts Payable** reports under **Reports** to see a detailed aging breakdown.

<Note>
If you need to partially pay an invoice, enable **Partial Payment** in **Accounting Settings**. Without this setting, Frappe Books requires the payment amount to equal the full outstanding amount of each linked invoice.
</Note>

## Cancel a payment

To cancel a submitted payment, open it and click **Cancel**. Frappe Books:
- Creates reversing ledger entries to undo the original posting.
- Restores the outstanding amount on each linked invoice.
- Updates the party's outstanding balance.

<Warning>
Cancelling a payment that was automatically created when an invoice was submitted will also reopen the invoice's outstanding amount. You may need to re-apply the payment or submit a new one.
</Warning>
