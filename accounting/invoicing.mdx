---
title: "Invoicing"
description: "Create, submit, and manage Sales Invoices and Purchase Invoices in Frappe Books."
---

Frappe Books has two types of invoices:

- **Sales Invoice** — issued to a customer when you sell goods or services. Uses number series `SINV-` by default.
- **Purchase Invoice** — received from a supplier when you buy goods or services. Uses number series `PINV-` by default.

Both invoice types are submittable documents. Posting an invoice creates ledger entries, updates the outstanding amount on the party, and makes the invoice available for payment reconciliation.

## Invoice statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | Invoice is saved but not posted to the ledger. You can edit all fields. |
| **Submitted** | Invoice is posted. Ledger entries have been created. Outstanding amount is tracked. |
| **Paid** | The outstanding amount has been fully cleared by one or more payments. |
| **Cancelled** | The invoice has been reversed. All linked payments are also cancelled. |

## Create a Sales Invoice

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Sales Invoice">
    Go to **Sales** in the sidebar and click **Sales Invoice**, then click **New**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a customer">
    In the **Customer** field, type to search and select the customer. The customer must have **Role** set to `Customer` or `Both` in their Party record.

    When you select a customer, Frappe Books automatically sets:
    - The **Account** field to the customer's default receivable account (typically **Debtors**).
    - The **Currency** to the customer's configured currency.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the invoice date">
    The **Date** field defaults to today. Change it if you need to back-date or forward-date the invoice.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add line items">
    In the **Items** table, add one row per product or service:

    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Item** | Link to an Item record. Populates description, rate, and account automatically. |
    | **Description** | Free-text description of the item or service. |
    | **Qty** | Quantity (computed from Transfer Quantity and Conversion Factor). |
    | **Rate** | Unit price of the item. |
    | **Tax** | Link to a Tax Template. The template's rates are applied to calculate the tax amount. |
    | **Amount** | Calculated as `Rate × Qty`. Read-only. |
    | **Account** | The income account to post this line to (e.g. Sales, Service Revenue). |

    <Tip>
    Click the expand icon on any line item row to open the quick-edit panel, where you can also set **Discount Amount**, **Discount Percent**, batch details, and HSN/SAC code.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review totals">
    Below the items table, Frappe Books shows:

    - **Net Total** — sum of all line item amounts before tax.
    - **Taxes** — tax summary table with account name, rate, and amount for each tax component.
    - **Grand Total** — Net Total plus taxes, minus any invoice-level discount.
    - **Base Grand Total** — Grand Total converted to the company's base currency (shown only when the invoice currency differs from the company currency).
    - **Outstanding Amount** — amount remaining unpaid.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply a discount (optional)">
    Invoice-level discounts are available when discounting is enabled in **Accounting Settings**. You can apply:

    - **Discount Percent** — reduces the grand total by a percentage.
    - **Discount Amount** — reduces the grand total by a fixed amount.
    - **Apply Discount After Tax** — when checked, the discount is applied to the taxed total rather than the pre-tax total.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set optional references">
    In the **References** section you can add:

    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Notes** | Free-text terms, payment instructions, or notes for the customer. |
    | **Attachment** | Attach a contract, purchase order, or other supporting document. |
    | **Quote Reference** | Link this invoice back to the originating Sales Quote. |
    | **Return Against** | Set when this invoice is a credit note returning a previous invoice. |

  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure submission settings (optional)">
    In the **Settings** tab:

    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Make Payment On Submit** | When checked, Frappe Books automatically creates and submits a payment for the full outstanding amount when you submit the invoice. Requires a default payment account to be set in Defaults. |
    | **Make Shipment On Submit** | When checked (and inventory is enabled), Frappe Books automatically creates a Shipment document on submission. |
    | **Apply Discount After Tax** | Moves discount calculation to after-tax totals. |

  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the invoice">
    Click **Submit**. Frappe Books:
    - Posts ledger entries (debits Debtors, credits the income account for each line, credits tax accounts).
    - Sets the **Outstanding Amount** to the Grand Total.
    - Updates the customer's outstanding balance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Create a Purchase Invoice

The flow for Purchase Invoices mirrors Sales Invoices with these differences:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Sales Invoice">
    - Party field label: **Customer**
    - Party role filter: `Customer` or `Both`
    - Account: Receivable account (Debtors)
    - Number series: `SINV-`
    - Items table target: `SalesInvoiceItem`
    - On submit: debits Debtors, credits income accounts
    - Linked transfer: Shipment
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Purchase Invoice">
    - Party field label: **Supplier**
    - Party role filter: `Supplier` or `Both`
    - Account: Payable account (Creditors)
    - Number series: `PINV-`
    - Items table target: `PurchaseInvoiceItem`
    - On submit: debits expense/stock accounts, credits Creditors
    - Linked transfer: Purchase Receipt
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Purchase Invoice">
    Go to **Purchases** in the sidebar and click **Purchase Invoice**, then click **New**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the supplier">
    Choose the supplier from the **Supplier** field. The payable account (Creditors) is set automatically from the supplier's default account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the invoice date">
    Use the date from the supplier's invoice. The **Date** field defaults to today.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add line items">
    Add items in the same way as a Sales Invoice. The **Account** column should point to an expense or stock account (e.g. Purchases, Cost of Goods Sold).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Click **Submit** to post the invoice. Frappe Books debits your expense accounts and credits Creditors.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Invoice field reference

| Field | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `name` | Data | Invoice number (read-only, auto-generated from number series) |
| `numberSeries` | Link | Number series used to generate the invoice number |
| `party` | Link | Customer (Sales) or Supplier (Purchase) |
| `account` | Link | Receivable or Payable account for the party |
| `date` | Datetime | Invoice posting date |
| `priceList` | Link | Price list to source item rates from (optional) |
| `items` | Table | Line items |
| `netTotal` | Currency | Total before tax (computed) |
| `taxes` | Table | Tax summary (computed) |
| `grandTotal` | Currency | Total after tax and discount (computed) |
| `baseGrandTotal` | Currency | Grand total in company currency (shown for multi-currency) |
| `outstandingAmount` | Currency | Unpaid amount remaining (computed) |
| `discountPercent` | Float | Invoice-level discount percentage |
| `discountAmount` | Currency | Invoice-level discount amount |
| `discountAfterTax` | Check | Apply discount after tax calculation |
| `makeAutoPayment` | Check | Create payment automatically on submit |
| `terms` | Text | Notes / payment terms |
| `attachment` | Attachment | Supporting document |
| `returnAgainst` | Link | Original invoice this return is linked to |

## Record a return or credit note

To issue a credit note (Sales) or debit note (Purchase), open the original submitted invoice and use the **Return** action from the actions menu. Frappe Books:

1. Creates a new invoice of the same type with negative quantities.
2. Sets **Return Against** to the original invoice number.
3. Calculates the return amounts based on quantities not yet returned.

Submit the return invoice to post the reversing ledger entries and reduce the original invoice's outstanding amount.

<Note>
You cannot cancel a submitted invoice that has linked return invoices. Cancel or delete the return invoices first.
</Note>

## Link a payment to an invoice

You can link payments to invoices in two ways:

1. **From the invoice**: After submission, click the **Make Payment** button (or use the action menu) to open a new payment pre-filled with the invoice details and outstanding amount.
2. **From the payment form**: In the **Payment Reference** (`for`) table on the Payment form, add a row linking to the invoice by type and name.

Each linked payment reduces the invoice's **Outstanding Amount**. When the outstanding amount reaches zero, the invoice status changes to **Paid**.

## Print an invoice

Frappe Books generates a printable view of submitted invoices. To print:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the submitted invoice">
    Navigate to the invoice you want to print.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the print icon">
    Use the print action from the top-right actions area of the form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a print template">
    Frappe Books uses **Print Templates** defined under **Setup > Print Settings**. Select the template and export to PDF.
  </Step>
</Steps>
