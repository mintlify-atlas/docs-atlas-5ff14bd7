---
title: "Profit and Loss"
description: "Summarize revenues and expenses to see business profitability"
---

The Profit and Loss (P&L) report shows your business's financial performance over a period of time. It compares all income earned against all expenses incurred to produce a net profit or net loss figure. Use it to understand whether your business is generating surplus or running a deficit in any given period.

## Navigate to the report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Reports">
    In the sidebar, click **Reports**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Profit and Loss">
    Under the Accounting section, click **Profit And Loss**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the period and run">
    Choose your date range and periodicity, then click **Run**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Selecting a period

The P&L report organizes amounts into date-range columns so you can compare performance across periods side by side.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="From Date / To Date" icon="calendar">
    Define the overall reporting window. All transactions within this range are included.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Periodicity" icon="chart-bar">
    Break the window into **Monthly**, **Quarterly**, or **Yearly** columns for trend analysis.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  The report uses your fiscal year settings to align periods correctly. Verify your fiscal year start date under **Settings > Accounting Settings** if periods appear misaligned.
</Note>

## Reading the report

The P&L is split into two root sections. Each section shows a hierarchy of accounts that you can expand or collapse.

### Income section

Lists all income and revenue accounts with amounts credited during the period. The section total represents your **gross income**.

### Expense section

Lists all expense accounts with amounts debited during the period. The section total represents your **total expenses**.

### Net profit / loss

The final row shows the difference between total income and total expenses:

```
Net Profit / (Loss) = Total Income − Total Expenses
```

A positive value means your business is profitable for the period. A negative value indicates a net loss.

<Tip>
  Use the periodicity columns to spot trends — for example, months where expenses spiked or income dropped — and drill into the General Ledger for those specific accounts.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  The P&L only reflects periods where transactions exist. If an account shows no activity in a column, it appears as zero rather than being hidden. A balanced Trial Balance is a prerequisite for accurate P&L figures — run it first if you suspect discrepancies.
</Warning>

## Export the report

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="CSV">
    Click **Export** in the toolbar and select **CSV** to download the full P&L data, including all periods and account rows, as a spreadsheet.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="PDF">
    Click **Export** and select **PDF** to produce a formatted, print-ready Profit and Loss statement.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
