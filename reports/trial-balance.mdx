---
title: "Trial Balance"
description: "Verify that debits equal credits across all accounts"
---

The Trial Balance is a verification report that lists the debit and credit totals for every account in your chart of accounts. Its purpose is to confirm that your books are mathematically in balance — a necessary condition before you generate a Profit and Loss statement or Balance Sheet.

<Note>
  A balanced Trial Balance does not guarantee that transactions were posted to the *correct* accounts, but it does confirm that no debit was posted without a matching credit.
</Note>

## Navigate to the report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Reports">
    In the sidebar, click **Reports**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Trial Balance">
    Under the Accounting section, click **Trial Balance**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the date range">
    Enter a **From Date** and **To Date**. Both fields are required. The report defaults to the current fiscal year.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the report">
    Click **Run** to generate the trial balance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Report columns

The Trial Balance splits balances into three time segments — opening, period activity, and closing — each with a debit and credit column.

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| **Account** | The account name from your chart of accounts. |
| **Opening (Dr)** | Cumulative debit balance for the account before the From Date. |
| **Opening (Cr)** | Cumulative credit balance for the account before the From Date. |
| **Debit** | Total debits posted to the account during the selected period. |
| **Credit** | Total credits posted to the account during the selected period. |
| **Closing (Dr)** | Running debit total through the To Date (Opening Dr + period Debit). |
| **Closing (Cr)** | Running credit total through the To Date (Opening Cr + period Credit). |

Accounts are grouped by root type — Assets, Liabilities, Income, Expenses, and Equity — with subtotals for each group.

## What to look for

A balanced Trial Balance shows equal grand totals in the **Closing (Dr)** and **Closing (Cr)** columns.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Balanced" icon="circle-check">
    Closing debits equal closing credits. Your books are in order and you can proceed to generate the P&L and Balance Sheet with confidence.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Not balanced" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Closing debits do not equal closing credits. There is a data entry error that must be resolved before relying on any other financial report.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What to do if it does not balance

<Steps>
  <Step title="Note the difference">
    Record the exact difference between the total closing debits and closing credits.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Identify the affected account">
    Look for accounts where the debit and credit columns appear inconsistent with the account type. For example, an expense account showing a net credit balance is unusual and worth investigating.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the General Ledger">
    Navigate to **Reports > General Ledger** and filter by the suspect account. Review individual entries for missing offsets or incorrect amounts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Correct the entry">
    Post a correcting Journal Entry or fix the source document that caused the imbalance. Re-run the Trial Balance to confirm it now balances.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Do not export or share your Profit and Loss or Balance Sheet until the Trial Balance is confirmed balanced. Both reports derive their figures from the same ledger data, so a discrepancy in the Trial Balance will propagate into those reports.
</Warning>

## Optional: hide group amounts

Enable the **Hide Group Amounts** filter to show only leaf-level account rows without rolled-up subtotals. This is useful when you want a flat list of individual accounts for reconciliation purposes.

## Export the report

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="CSV">
    Click **Export** and select **CSV** to download the full trial balance including all account rows and period columns.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="PDF">
    Click **Export** and select **PDF** for a formatted version suitable for sharing with an accountant or auditor.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
