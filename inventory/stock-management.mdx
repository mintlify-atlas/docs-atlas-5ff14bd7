---
title: "Stock Management"
description: "Track inventory with stock movements, shipments, and purchase receipts"
---

Frappe Books tracks the quantity and value of physical goods across one or more storage locations. Every movement—receiving goods from a supplier, shipping to a customer, or transferring between warehouses—creates a **Stock Ledger Entry** that updates your inventory balances in real time.

<Note>
  Enable inventory tracking before using these features. Go to **Settings → Inventory Settings** and turn on **Enable Inventory**.
</Note>

## Locations

A **Location** is a named storage place—a warehouse, store room, shelf, or counter. Stock quantities are tracked per location so you always know where each item is held.

To create a location, go to **Inventory → Locations** and click **New**. Enter a unique name and optionally link an address. Set your most-used location as the **Default Location** in Inventory Settings.

<Tip>
  Create separate locations for each physical space you track independently—for example, a main warehouse, a retail store floor, and a returns area.
</Tip>

## Stock movements

A **Stock Movement** records an internal inventory adjustment without a corresponding supplier or customer transaction. Use it for opening stock entries, internal consumption, manufacturing, or warehouse transfers.

Stock movements are submittable. Submitting one generates Stock Ledger Entries that update your inventory balances.

### Movement types

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="Material Receipt" icon="arrow-down-to-line">
    Records goods received into a location without a supplier invoice—for example, opening stock or manufacturing output. Requires a **To** location on each item row.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Material Issue" icon="arrow-up-from-line">
    Records goods removed from a location without a customer invoice—for example, materials consumed in production or written off as damaged. Requires a **From** location on each item row.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Material Transfer" icon="arrows-left-right">
    Moves stock from one location to another within your business. Requires both a **From** and a **To** location on each item row.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Manufacture" icon="gear">
    Records a manufacturing operation that consumes raw materials (From location) and produces finished goods (To location).
  </Card>
</Columns>

### Create a stock movement

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Stock Movements">
    Go to **Inventory → Stock Movements** and click **New**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a movement type">
    Choose the **Movement Type** that describes what you are recording. The available **From** and **To** fields on each item row update accordingly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add item rows">
    Click **Add Row** in the Items table. Select the item, enter the quantity and rate, and set the From and/or To locations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Click **Submit**. Frappe Books validates the movement and creates the corresponding Stock Ledger Entries.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Purchase receipt

A **Purchase Receipt** records the physical arrival of goods from a supplier. It is separate from the Purchase Invoice—goods arrive via a receipt, while the invoice records the financial obligation to pay. Link the two using the **Back Reference** field.

Submitting a Purchase Receipt increases stock levels at the target location for each item row.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Key fields">

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Party** | The supplier from whom goods are being received. Required. |
| **Date** | The date goods were physically received. |
| **Back Reference** | Links to the corresponding Purchase Invoice. |
| **Return Against** | Links to the original Purchase Receipt (for returns only). |

  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Steps">

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the Purchase Receipt">
    Go to **Inventory → Purchase Receipts** and click **New**. Select the supplier in the **Party** field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Link the invoice">
    Set the **Back Reference** to the corresponding Purchase Invoice.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add items">
    Add the items received with their quantities, locations, and rates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Click **Submit**. Stock levels at the target location increase by the quantities entered.
  </Step>
</Steps>

  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Shipment

A **Shipment** records goods dispatched to a customer. Like Purchase Receipts, Shipments are separate from Sales Invoices and link via the **Back Reference** field.

Submitting a Shipment decreases stock levels at the source location for each item row.

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Party** | The customer to whom goods are being shipped. Required. |
| **Date** | The dispatch date. |
| **Back Reference** | Links to the corresponding Sales Invoice. |
| **Return Against** | Links to the original Shipment (for returns only). |

<Note>
  When you complete a POS sale for inventory items, Frappe Books automatically creates and submits a Shipment from the configured POS warehouse. You do not need to create one manually in that case.
</Note>

## Batch and serial number tracking

For items that require lot or unit-level traceability, Frappe Books supports two tracking modes:

- **Batch tracking** — group multiple units into a named batch (e.g., a production lot or expiry date group). Enable **Has Batch** on the item and turn on **Enable Batches** in Inventory Settings.
- **Serial number tracking** — assign a unique identifier to every individual unit. Enable **Has Serial Number** on the item and turn on **Enable Serial Number** in Inventory Settings.

Every stock transaction for a tracked item must specify the batch or serial number. Serial number statuses update automatically: `Inactive` when not yet received, `Active` when in stock, and `Delivered` when shipped.

<Warning>
  Batch and serial number settings on an item are permanent once saved. Plan your tracking requirements before creating items at scale.
</Warning>
